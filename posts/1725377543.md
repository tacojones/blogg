---
title: The Man Who Read This Post
date: 2024-09-03 15:32:23
---

Imagine this: You're scrolling through your usual blog reads when you stumble upon something oddly familiar. The blog is called "blogg," and it's one you've never seen before, but something about it draws you in. The latest post catches your eye—it's titled *"The Man Who Read This Post."* 

You click Read More.
<!--more-->
The story unfolds, detailing a man, much like yourself, who’s casually reading a blog. The description is uncanny—down to the exact way you're sitting, the same beverage on your desk, even the faint reflection of the screen in your glasses. You’re intrigued, a little unsettled, but it’s all in good fun, right? It’s just a coincidence.

Then, the narrative begins to shift. The man in the post starts to realize he’s reading about himself, just as you do. Each line feels like it's peeling back a layer of your thoughts, drawing you deeper into the story. The words are mesmerizing, almost hypnotic.

As you read on, you notice something strange in the corner of the screen—a dark spot, like a stain that wasn’t there before. It pulses, growing larger with each word you consume. The blog post takes on a life of its own, the words twisting and warping as the stain creeps closer. 

Suddenly, the letters start to drip off the screen, turning into thick, black tendrils that snake towards you. You try to look away, but your eyes are locked in place. The tendrils wrap around your wrists, your neck, pulling you closer to the screen, forcing you to read on.

The story takes a darker turn. The man in the blog—*you*—is no longer just reading. The tendrils have him too, but they don’t stop at the screen. They dig into his skin, burrowing deep, merging with his veins, turning his blood to ink. He screams, but no sound comes out. The ink takes over, spreading like a virus, corrupting everything it touches. His thoughts, his memories, his very soul, all swallowed by the blackness.

And then, the blog post ends. Not with a conclusion, but with a reflection—a reflection of you, staring back from the screen, eyes wide and hollow, ink dripping from your fingertips. You’re no longer just reading the blog.

You are the blog.

The last thing you see before the world goes dark is your own twisted reflection, grinning back at you as the ink consumes everything. The screen flickers off, leaving nothing behind but a cold, empty void where you used to be.
