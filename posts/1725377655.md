### **Overview**
In this tabletop RPG, players step into the shoes of The Dude, Walter, and Donnie as they navigate the bizarre and chaotic world of Los Angeles in the early '90s. The game mixes elements of mystery, humor, and absurdity, drawing heavily from the movie's iconic moments and characters. The primary antagonists are the nihilists, who are determined to wreak havoc and claim what they believe is rightfully theirs.
<!--more-->
### **Game Setup**

- **Number of Players:** 3-5
- **Game Master (GM):** One player assumes the role of the GM, controlling the story, setting, and non-player characters (NPCs).
- **Playable Characters:**
  - **The Dude:** The laid-back, peace-loving slacker who prefers a simple life of bowling and White Russians. He excels in charm, negotiation, and non-violent conflict resolution.
  - **Walter Sobchak:** A hot-headed, paranoid Vietnam veteran with a strict moral code. He is skilled in combat, intimidation, and tactics.
  - **Donnie Kerabatsos:** A soft-spoken, somewhat oblivious bowler who excels in luck-based actions and has a knack for being in the right place at the wrong time.

### **Character Sheets**

Each character has a character sheet detailing their stats, abilities, and special items. Key attributes include:

- **Coolness:** How well the character keeps their composure.
- **Charm:** Ability to influence others through conversation or charisma.
- **Combat:** Proficiency in physical confrontations.
- **Luck:** How often random events work in the character’s favor.
- **Resourcefulness:** Ability to solve problems and think on their feet.

**Unique Abilities:**
- **The Dude:** "Abide" (reroll any failed coolness check once per session), "White Russian" (improve charm in social encounters when drinking).
- **Walter:** "Over the Line!" (intimidate or scare off a single opponent), "This is What Happens!" (reroll a combat check once per session).
- **Donnie:** "Mark it Zero!" (automatically succeed on any luck-based action once per session), "What’s Going On?" (gain an extra piece of information from the GM when confused).

### **Villains: The Nihilists**
The primary antagonists, the nihilists, are driven by a nihilistic philosophy and a desire to cause chaos. They have their own set of attributes and abilities that the GM can use to challenge the players.

- **Franz:** The leader, skilled in manipulation and strategy.
- **Uli:** The enforcer, strong and ruthless in combat.
- **Kieffer:** The wildcard, prone to unpredictable and dangerous actions.

**Nihilist Abilities:**
- **Chaos:** Disrupts the players' plans, forcing them to adapt.
- **Fear:** Lowers the players' coolness or combat skills when confronted.
- **Deception:** Creates false leads or misleads the players.

### **Gameplay Mechanics**

- **Dice Rolling:** The game uses a d6 system, where players roll a six-sided die to determine the outcome of actions. Success is determined by rolling equal to or higher than a target number set by the GM.
- **Skill Checks:** Players roll for actions using their attributes, adding modifiers based on their character's abilities. For example, The Dude might roll Coolness +1 to talk his way out of trouble.
- **Resource Management:** Players can collect and use items such as White Russians, bowling balls, or Walter's gun, each providing different bonuses or effects.

### **Game Progression**
The game unfolds in a series of scenes, with the GM setting the stage. Players navigate various challenges, such as tracking down the missing ransom money, dealing with confrontations at the bowling alley, or escaping the nihilists' traps. The game balances humor with tension, allowing for creative problem-solving and character interactions.

### **Win Condition**
The game can have multiple possible endings, depending on how the players handle the challenges. The ultimate goal is to outmaneuver the nihilists and restore The Dude's laid-back lifestyle, but the journey is full of twists and turns that could lead to hilarious or disastrous outcomes.

### **Drinking Game Aspect**
Incorporating a drinking game element, players can take a drink whenever:
- The Dude says, "The Dude abides."
- Walter loses his temper.
- Donnie gets confused or says something irrelevant.
- A nihilist makes an absurd demand.

This adds a layer of fun and chaos to the game, keeping players engaged and immersed in the world of *The Big Lebowski*.
